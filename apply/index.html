Quick logistics

What you're signing up for.

  • 10 weeks One 90-minute live session per week, plus 4–6 hours of independent work. Roughly 60–80 hours total.
  • ~50 participants Deliberately mixed across technical and non-technical roles and across units at U‑M.
  • Three lead instructors Plus guest speakers from across U‑M and the broader field when their expertise bears on the week's topic.
  • Three team projects Tier-1 IT Help Desk triage. HR onboarding vendor evaluation. A capstone on a real U‑M service.

FAQ

Questions we expect you to have.

How much coding is involved?

Some hands-on work with AI tools, which builders in the cohort tend to enjoy. Coding is optional throughout; roughly half of each cohort comes from non-technical roles and does the course without writing any. The focus is on the systems around the code: services, data, governance, and judgment.

I'm in a service or operations role, not a technical one. Will this work for me?

Yes — you're part of the core audience. The course is designed to bring service managers, business analysts, operations leads, data stewards, and front-line staff into the same cohort as developers and IT leads, because real AI work at a university needs all of those roles at the same table. See the Who it's for page for the full role-by-role pitch.

How much time per week does this really take?

The live session is 90 minutes each week. Expect 4–6 hours of independent work on top of that — readings, weekly assignments anchored in your own service, and team project time. Some weeks will run lighter; a few (around project deadlines) will run heavier.

Do I need approval from my manager?

Most units welcome staff participating in professional development that produces tangible work artifacts. That said, a 10-week commitment does deserve an honest conversation with your manager. If a briefing document would help — something you can forward that summarizes what you'll produce and how it benefits your unit — ask us and we'll send one.

What are the conditions on the $1,500 award?

The award is offered when funding is available, contingent on HR, finance, and OGC review, and paid to participants who complete the course with a score of 87% or higher and meet participation expectations. Some cohorts may run without the award depending on funding. Final terms are communicated in writing to admitted participants. The full conditions are stated below.

What if I have to miss a session?

Attendance matters — the cohort format depends on it — but missing one session across ten weeks is not course-ending. Missing several will affect your participation score and may affect completion-award eligibility. The course is not self-paced, and recorded replays are not a substitute for showing up to the live session and your team's work.

What will I actually produce?

By Week 10: a service map of a real service you work with, a data-flow diagram of the same service, a responsible AI proposal (current state, future state, risk assessment, accessibility plan, human-oversight model, implementation roadmap), a vendor evaluation package, and a final reflection written to a colleague who did not take the course. Most participants leave with material their unit actually uses.

Will I get a certificate?

Participants who complete the course receive a completion credential recorded in U‑M professional development systems (the specific recording path is still being finalized with HR and ITS). The course is not for academic credit.

Can I use AI tools on the coursework?

Yes — with discipline. Several assignments explicitly ask you to use AI tools and then evaluate their outputs. But you'll also be expected to follow the course's own rules: never use real sensitive data with a tool that shouldn't see it; document what the tool did and didn't do; own the output. The goal is to leave knowing how to use AI honestly, not to outsource your thinking to it.

What data can I use for my assignments?

Preferably non-sensitive or synthesized data from a service you work with. The course will teach you how to reason about classification and what's appropriate for third-party tools vs. U‑M-sanctioned tools vs. no-AI-at-all; we'd rather you err on the side of synthetic or de-identified data than take a risk with real institutional data while you're learning.

Who teaches the course?

The course is hosted by University of Michigan Information and Technology Services and led by three instructors with practical experience in service design, institutional data, and responsible AI. Multiple guest speakers — from U‑M units and from the broader field — join sessions across the term when their expertise bears directly on the week's topic. Instructor and guest names for the next cohort are announced with cohort dates.

How is the cohort selected?

We read every application. We are trying to assemble a cohort that is mixed across roles, units, and experience levels with AI — which means we may ask you to wait a cohort if the current one is already heavy in your area. The goal is a group that teaches each other as much as the course teaches them.

Accessibility

Built to include you.

The course teaches and is built around WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards. Live sessions include captioning; course materials are provided in accessible formats by default. If you need a specific accommodation — or want to ask about one before applying — email its-ai-cohort@umich.edu and we'll work with you. The same applies to this website: if any content is hard to use with assistive technology or in an alternate format, please tell us.

Completion award — full conditions

The $1,500 award, in full.

$1,500

Participants who complete the course with a final score of 87% or higher, complete all required assignments and major projects, and meet participation expectations are eligible for a $1,500 professional development completion award.

Eligibility is not guaranteed and is subject to final University of Michigan program rules, funding availability, applicable HR, finance, and tax review, and administrative approval by the program sponsor and relevant offices. The award may be subject to applicable taxes and reporting requirements. The course may be offered with or without the completion award depending on funding availability in any given cohort. Final terms will be communicated in writing to admitted participants.
